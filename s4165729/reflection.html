Reflection

Questions twelve and thirteen close the analysis with a personal evaluation, identifying what was most frustrating and most satisfying about the interaction and explaining the reasoning behind each response.

12

Most Frustrating Aspect

What is the most frustrating part of the interaction and what makes that part frustrating?

The most frustrating aspect of the interaction is the limited use of explicit signifiers and navigation cues, which can make it difficult to immediately understand how to move between sections. While the minimal design supports aesthetic clarity, it can reduce usability for first-time users by requiring additional effort to interpret interactive elements. This creates a tension between visual simplicity and functional clarity.

13

Most Satisfying Aspect

What is the most satisfying part of the interaction and what makes that part satisfying?

The most satisfying aspect is the seamless integration of interaction and visual design, particularly through smooth scrolling and consistent layout. The experience feels cohesive and intentional, with each element contributing to a unified aesthetic. This level of polish enhances usability and reinforces the designer's professional identity, making the interaction both engaging and visually rewarding.
