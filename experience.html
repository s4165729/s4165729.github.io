Interactive Media — Assignment 1

Portfolio Analysis
Rich Brown

Akira Lordsmith

Experience

An exploration of the Rich Brown portfolio as an interactive digital experience, analysing how design, movement, and visual elements shape user engagement and meaning. By engaging directly with the site, this analysis examines how interaction guides user behaviour, communicates purpose, and creates an emotional response.

05

Primary Goal

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal is to communicate Rich Brown's creative identity and professional capability through a curated digital portfolio. The design presents his work in an accessible, visually engaging way that informs and persuades potential collaborators or employers.

06

Communicating the Goal

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

Minimal interface design, strong visual hierarchy, and careful interaction design direct attention to the projects. A restrained colour palette, clean typography, and scrolling interaction guide the user through the portfolio content.

07

Interaction Over Time

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

Designed for short, repeat interactions rather than extended use. Users can quickly navigate and consume content but may return multiple times for deeper exploration or comparison.

08

Signalling Temporal Use

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

The absence of accounts or progress tracking and the clear navigation structure signal that users can re-enter at any point without prior context. This promotes flexible, repeat engagement.

09

Media References

What other media forms does the experience reference?

References include editorial design, digital portfolios, high-end fashion websites, and print media such as magazines, evident in structured layouts, whitespace, and typographic emphasis.

10

References and Behaviour

What does this reference communicate about how you should act when engaging with the experience?

Users are encouraged to explore slowly and thoughtfully, similar to reading a curated magazine or visiting a gallery, paying attention to visual and conceptual details.

11

References and Feeling

What does this reference communicate about how you should feel when engaging with the experience?

The references evoke calm, focus, and sophistication. The minimal aesthetic reduces visual noise, enhancing immersion and attention, reinforcing the professionalism of the portfolio.
