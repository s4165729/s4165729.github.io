Experience

Questions five through eleven shift from observation toward interpretation, examining the portfolio's goals, how it communicates them, and what cultural references it draws on to shape user behaviour and feeling.

05

Primary Goal

What is your impression of the intended primary goal of the interactive experience?

The primary goal of the experience is to communicate Rich Brown's creative identity and professional capability through a curated digital portfolio. The site is designed to present his work in a way that is both accessible and visually engaging, positioning him within a contemporary design context. This aligns with the broader function of portfolio websites, which aim to both inform and persuade audiences such as potential employers or collaborators of the designer's value.

06

Communicating the Goal

How does the interactive experience communicate this primary goal?

The experience communicates this goal through a combination of minimal interface design, strong visual hierarchy, and controlled interaction design. The restrained colour palette and clean typography reduce cognitive load, ensuring that attention is directed toward the projects themselves. The use of scrolling as a primary interaction creates a guided narrative flow, while spacing and layout reinforce the importance of each project.

07

Interaction Over Time

What is your impression of how the experience should be interacted with over time?

The experience appears to be designed for short, repeat interactions rather than extended continuous use. Users can quickly navigate and consume content, but the structure also supports revisiting specific projects over time. This aligns with portfolio-based experiences, where engagement is often task-oriented such as reviewing work, but may occur multiple times for deeper exploration or comparison.

08

Signalling Temporal Use

How does the interactive experience communicate how it should be interacted with over time?

This is communicated through the absence of long-term engagement features such as accounts or progress tracking, alongside a clear and intuitive navigation structure. The simplicity of the interface and immediate access to content suggest that users can re-enter the experience at any point without prior context. This reduces friction and reinforces the idea of flexible, repeat engagement.

09

Media References

What other media forms does the experience reference?

The experience draws from editorial design, digital portfolios, and high-end fashion websites, as well as elements of print media such as magazines. This is evident in the use of structured layouts, intentional whitespace, and typographic emphasis, all of which reflect traditional editorial composition adapted into a digital format.

10

References and Behaviour

What does this reference communicate about how you should act when engaging with the experience?

These media references encourage a mode of interaction that is slow, deliberate, and observational. Similar to engaging with a curated magazine or gallery, the user is positioned to explore content thoughtfully, paying attention to visual and conceptual details. This reduces impulsive interaction and instead promotes a more reflective engagement with the work.

11

References and Feeling

What does this reference communicate about how you should feel when engaging with the experience?

The references evoke a sense of calm, focus, and sophistication. The minimal aesthetic and editorial influence reduce visual noise, allowing the user to feel more immersed and attentive. This emotional response enhances the perceived professionalism of the work and strengthens the overall user experience.
