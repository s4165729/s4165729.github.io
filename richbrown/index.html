Research

An exploration of the Rich Brown portfolio as an interactive digital experience, analysing how design, movement, and visual elements shape user engagement and meaning. By engaging directly with the site, this analysis examines how interaction guides user behaviour, communicates purpose, and creates an emotional response.

01

First Impression

What was the first thing you paid attention to when interacting with the experience?

When entering the Rich Brown Portfolio, the first element that drew my attention was the animated loading sequence, where percentages and fragmented text rapidly appeared across the screen. This acted as an immediate signifier of interactivity, indicating that the experience is dynamic rather than static. The muted grey-blue colour palette and minimal typography further established a refined visual identity, directing focus toward content while creating a calm and controlled first impression.

02

Discrete Actions

Spend two minutes with the experience and create a list of each of your discrete actions.

Entered the website and observed the loading animation Interpreted percentage indicators as progress feedback (system status) Scrolled vertically to navigate the homepage Paused to read typographic content Hovered to identify interactive affordances Clicked into project-based sections Explored individual project layouts Analysed spatial hierarchy and composition Navigated back to the main page

03

Most Engaged Section

What part of the experience did you spend the most time engaging with?

I spent the most time engaging with the project sections, as they function as the core content and primary communicative focus of the portfolio. These sections required sustained attention due to their layered presentation of visual and textual information. The structured layout and emphasis on individual works reinforced their importance within the overall hierarchy, encouraging deeper interaction and supporting he site’s goal of showcasing the designer’s capabilities.

04

Most Common Action

What was the most common action in your two minute interaction with the experience?

The most frequent action was scrolling, which acts as the dominant navigation mechanism. This continuous vertical interaction creates a fluid and immersive experience, reinforcing a sense of progression through the content.
